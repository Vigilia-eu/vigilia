Europe's night watch

While Europe argues,
narratives move.

Russia runs influence networks at millions of accounts across dozens of countries. They seed distrust at the fringe and watch it climb to the mainstream. They have been doing this for fifteen years. No one is sufficiently aware of the scale or the urgency.

What we're doing See it running
How it works

The attack follows a reproducible four-stage cycle. Intervening at Stage 1 or 2 costs ten times less than responding at Stage 4 — by then it is already the news.

Stage 1

Seed

Fringe channels, fake local infrastructure, Telegram dark channels. Extreme, untraceable, deniable.

Stage 2

Amplify

Bot networks create false consensus. Engagement metrics are manufactured to signal "this is what people think."

Stage 3

Bridge

Political influencers and sympathetic journalists pick it up. The source chain breaks — the narrative travels without its origin.

Stage 4

Mainstream

A politician cites it. A newspaper covers it. It is now the news. Intervention here is ten times more expensive.

Recent examples: Wagner / Africa / migration narratives seeded on Telegram, amplified via far-right accounts, and bridged into mainstream debate within days. This is the pattern. It is not new. It is the scale that is.

The response

Watch. Speak. Federate.

You cannot silence a network that reconstitutes faster than it is removed. You can balance the playing field. Not with the same tactics — with something they cannot manufacture: authentic presence at scale.
