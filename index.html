Europe's night watch

While Europe argues,
narratives move.

Coordinated influence operations have been running across Europe for fifteen years — seeding distrust at the fringe and watching it climb to the mainstream. Vigilia is the counter-system: detect, respond, spread, and document at continental scale.

How it works Open the research library

The attack follows a reproducible four-stage cycle: seed a narrative at the fringe, manufacture consensus with coordinated amplification, bridge it through political influencers and sympathetic outlets, and watch it enter the mainstream as news. By Stage 4, the cost of an effective response is ten times higher than at Stage 1.

No single organisation currently operates with the reach, speed, and editorial discipline to intervene at Stage 1–2 across all 27 EU member states simultaneously. That is what Vigilia is being built to do.

The system

Four parts. One loop.

Vigilia is not a single product. It is four interconnected layers that reinforce each other: reconnaissance feeds response, response feeds the network, and everything feeds the documented record.

Caput I · Explorator

Reconnaissance

Monitors public source tiers across Europe — from seedbed Telegram channels to mainstream outlets — and detects coordinated narratives at Stage 1–2, before they bridge to the press. Not a fact-checker. A scout. Active before the threat arrives.

The reconnaissance layer
Caput II · Machina

Editorial engine

Researchers and journalists bring the story. Machina writes, fact-checks, illustrates, and distributes at scale — every day, across every node, without a bottleneck. Real editorial voice. Real sourcing. Already running with live output.

The editorial engine
Caput III · Foederatio

Federated network

One engine, 27 country nodes. Content flows down to every hub and is re-voiced for its local audience. Intelligence flows back up — what Europe is reading, what narratives are gaining ground. A continental sensor grid and publishing network in one.

The federated network
Bibliotheca · Annales

Research library

A structured, evidence-tiered record of influence operations across Europe — documented, alleged, and suspected. Indexed by country, party, person, and timeline. Every claim carries its evidence. Every source is cited. The institutional memory of the system.
