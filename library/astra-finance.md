# Astra Europa — Finances Research Notes

## Overview

Astra Europa launched on **9 May 2026** — seven weeks before this document was written. As a movement in its first weeks, it publishes no financial statements and has disclosed virtually nothing about its funding. Everything below is inferred from public signals; treat it as a starting hypothesis, not a finding.

**Current status (June 2026):** Unknown. Almost certainly self-funded by founders/members, no institutional backing disclosed or claimed. Earlier-stage financially than Ave Europa was at a comparable point.

---

## Funding Model — What Can Be Inferred

No direct statements about funding exist in public sources. What we can read from indirect signals:

- **No "quit our jobs" language.** Unlike Ave's founders (Skrobisz and Wenzel both publicly stated they quit corporate jobs and invested personal savings), Astra's founding team **appears to hold full-time employment**. Skrobisz now works in business development at an aerospace company; Rolf Strijdhorst is a platform engineer at a logistics company; Ivo Ijssennagger builds data platforms for the Dutch energy grid; Matthias Matthiesen is a legal/tech executive. No founder has cited personal savings as the runway.
- **This cuts both ways.** Less personal-savings burn = more sustainable in the short term. But also fewer committed FTE hours = slower build.
- **Member-funded by design, likely.** The Volt and Ave blueprints they explicitly reference are both grassroots, donation-driven models. No reason to expect a different model here.
- **The Matthiesen signal.** Founding team member **Matthias Matthiesen** is a former Shopify board member — a profile that suggests significant personal capital and likely a strong investor/executive network. This does not mean he has invested in Astra, but it makes the team's access to early capital meaningfully different from Ave's.

## Risks & Unknowns

- **Near-total opacity.** Seven weeks in, no public financials, no donation page found, no legal registration confirmed. This is normal for a freshly launched movement but it means any financial assessment is essentially speculation.
- **No confirmed personal-capital commitment.** Ave's runway was explicit: two founders quit jobs and invested savings. Astra's founders appear employed. Unless someone is quietly funding it personally, the actual runway may be very thin — event costs, domain, basic tooling.
- **Governance transition ahead.** The Interim Steering Committee has a 6-month mandate (elected May 2026). How the movement funds that transition — membership drives, grants, major donor — is entirely open.
- **The Matthiesen upside.** If Matthiesen or his network has seeded early capital (likely discussed but not public), the financial picture could be much better than the zero-disclosure baseline suggests. Worth asking directly.
- **EU party registration as future lever.** Same as Ave: if Astra ever registers as a European political party, EU party/foundation grants become available. Not current status, but a meaningful future path — especially given their explicitly federalist-democratic positioning, which is cleanly aligned with EU institutional values.

## Implications for Vigilia

- **Option 3 (full partnership, Vigilia team embedded) is even less realistic now than for Ave** — no confirmed budget, no known runway, and the movement is 7 weeks old. This doesn't mean it's impossible, but it requires a confirmed funding source before the conversation can go there.
- **Option 2 (SaaS) is the realistic entry.** A SaaS fee sized for a bootstrapped team (not a funded party) is the credible starting point. Small, demonstrable, scalable up.
- **The Matthiesen network is the wildcard.** If he or someone in his orbit has already capitalized the movement, Option 3 becomes much more plausible much faster. The first meeting should try to read this signal.
- **Probe in the first meeting:**
  - Who is funding this, and how?
  - Has anyone on the team committed personal capital?
  - Is there a donate infrastructure in place, and at what scale?
  - What is the 6-month governance transition plan financially?
  - Any conversations with institutional backers or grant bodies yet?
- **Red-line risk is lower than Ave** — no founding-funder resignation, no far-right-drift incident. The reputational angle of a deep partnership is cleaner here. Financial risk is the live question, not reputational.
