# David Engels & Hesperialism — Reference Notes

> **Why this is here:** This is **Ave Europa's intellectual heritage, kept here for comparative context only.** David Engels appeared on the Ave Europa podcast (March 2026) as an intellectual reference for Ave's civilisational, Greco-Roman framing. **Astra Europa explicitly departed from this framing** — their manifesto contains no heritage or identity language; the register is liberal-democratic and technocratic, not civilisationist. Understanding Hesperialism helps map exactly where Astra drew the line and why their voice reads differently.

## Who is David Engels?
- Belgian historian and philosopher (b. 1979). PhD in Roman religion (RWTH Aachen).
- Specialist in ancient Roman history and **civilisational cycles** — best known for reading contemporary Europe through the lens of the **late Roman Republic** (rise-and-decline patterns).
- A leading voice of European **declinism / civilisational conservatism**.
- Key works: *Le Déclin* (EU ↔ late Roman Republic), *Que faire? Vivre avec le déclin de l'Europe*, *Renovatio Europae* (ed., 2019), *Defending Civilizational Europe: A Short Treatise on Hesperialism* (2024).

## What is Hesperialism?
A political ideology Engels formulated around **2019** — in the edited volume *Renovatio Europae* and the essay *"Towards a 'Hesperialist' Future for Europe"* (The European Conservative, May 2019).

- **The name:** "Hesperia" is the Greek term for the **uttermost Western part of the world** — i.e. the West / Europe.
- **The core idea:** (limited) European integration *out of respect for a shared past and shared future challenges* — **without** crushing local, regional, and national identities under "multiculturalism, individualism, turbo-capitalism, and globalisation."
- It is explicitly a **civilisational patriotism**: transcend narrow nationalism, but **preserve national sovereignty** — a confederation of sovereign states rooted in a common **Greco-Roman, Christian, and Enlightenment** heritage.

### The six pillars (Engels' own wording)
1. Defence of the natural family
2. Severe regulation of immigration
3. Return to Natural Law
4. Protection of a socially responsible economic model
5. Radical implementation of subsidiarity
6. Reinvigoration of the cultural roots of our identity

### Proposed political structure
Modelled on the **Holy Roman Empire's** principle of unity-without-uniformity:
- **Bicameral legislature** — European Parliament (lower house) + European Council (upper house).
- **European Commission dissolved**, replaced by nominated **Secretaries of State**.
- A **directly elected European president** for foreign policy and dispute arbitration.
- **Strictly delimited EU competencies**: defence, border security, infrastructure, legal harmonisation, and research only.

## Relation to Ave Europa
- **Direct touchpoint:** Engels was a guest on the Ave Europa podcast (March 2026) — he lends the movement intellectual / civilisational weight.
- **Shared ground:** Greco-Roman and civilisational heritage, centre-right values, the natural family, restrictive migration, subsidiarity, "renewal amid decline."
- **The key difference — structure:** Ave brands itself **federalist** (inspired by the Roman Republic and the US Founders). Engels' Hesperialism is **confederal / sovereigntist** and explicitly critiques *both* left-wing eurofederalism *and* right-wing nationalism as wrong turns. The affinity is on **values and civilisational narrative**, not on the federal-vs-confederal blueprint.
- **Takeaway for us:** Engels is best read as an **intellectual lodestar / fellow-traveller**, not a structural model for Ave. Useful for tone, references, and "depth" content — but cite with care, since his framing is sovereigntist rather than federalist.
