The source tiers

Where narratives begin.

A coordinated narrative doesn't start in a newspaper. It starts in the dark — seeded in fringe channels, amplified by bots, and only then picked up by journalists and politicians. Explorator watches every tier. It catches the pattern at the source.

How it works

Content-blind. Pattern-sharp.

Explorator does not read for political position. It reads for coordination. A narrative being pushed hard from many directions at once, faster than organic spread would explain — that is the signal, regardless of what the narrative says.

⚡

Velocity

How fast is a narrative spreading relative to organic baseline? Sudden acceleration across unrelated accounts is a coordination signal, not a news event.

⊕

Coordination

Are multiple accounts pushing the same framing at the same time? Cross-channel correlation surfaces manufactured consensus that no single channel reveals on its own.

↑

Escalation

Is the narrative climbing tiers — from seedbed to fringe to political? Tier-climbing at speed is the bridge pattern. Catching it here is the whole point.

Every cluster receives a combined risk score across all three dimensions. The score determines what happens next.

Detection as inoculation

Not correction. Prevention.

Debunking is reactive — you correct after the damage is done, and research shows people remember the claim, not the correction. Explorator makes a different move possible: inoculate against the manipulation technique before the narrative arrives.

The handoff: detection → inoculation

When Explorator detects a narrative seeding across independent channels in a narrow time window, the signal goes to a human analyst. If confirmed, it becomes a brief for journalists and partners — not "this story is false," but "this framing technique is being deployed right now, here is how to recognise it."

The reader who encounters the adversarial narrative has already seen the pattern exposed. That is inoculation. It works before the breakthrough, not after.

The discipline rules
  • Inoculate against the technique, not the claim. Expose the trick, don't amplify the rumour.
  • Detection output goes to analysts and partners only. Never a public alert that gives the narrative oxygen.
  • Flag coordinated amplification, not the political position — keeps Vigilia out of the "arbiter of truth" role.
  • Networks and patterns only. Not individuals.
  • Every assessment carries a readable rationale and an audit trail. No black-box verdicts.
The landscape

Many players. Five gaps.

Europe has organisations working on disinformation — some excellent. But mapping them against the four-stage attack cycle reveals five structural gaps that none of them fill. Those gaps are exactly what Explorator is designed for.

EU institutions

EUvsDisinfo · EDMO · ECDR

Real scale, real legitimacy. They debunk Russian propaganda, certify fact-checkers, and coordinate across member states. But slow by design — and the EU stamp is counterproductive with exactly the audiences you need to reach.

Research organisations

EU DisinfoLab · DFRLab · Bellingcat

Gold-standard investigative work and bot-network mapping. But investigations take weeks, and the strongest labs carry US or Atlantic Council associations — an attack surface in European politics.

Grassroots

NAFO · Lithuanian Elves · French Response

Extremely fast. NAFO attacks pro-Russian accounts with humour at scale; the Elves have tracked Baltic troll networks for years. But no quality control, predominantly English, and geographically limited.

National fact-checkers

Correctiv · Maldita · AFP · Pagella

Strong and local, some at high volume. But they correct case by case, after the fact. The model is reactive by definition — they cannot intervene at Stage 1.

Five structural gaps that no one fills
1
Stage 1–2 monitoring — no one watches the seedbed-to-fringe pipeline fast enough, in enough languages, at continental scale.
2
The journalist filter — no fast, trusted service lets a newsroom check before publication: where does this story come from, and is it coordinated?
3
Positive narrative absent — the entire landscape is defensive. No one systematically builds an authentic European story alongside the counter-work.
4
No independent European actor — strong research labs are US-linked; EU bodies carry the EU stamp. Both are attack surfaces. A genuinely European, independent actor does not exist.
5
Speed — nothing except NAFO and the French foreign ministry operates fast enough to intervene in the 24–48 hour window. Institutional processes are simply too slow.
Vigilia does not compete with this landscape. It completes it. Every player listed is a potential partner, data source, or distribution channel.
In the federation

One Explorator per node.

Every country node in the Vigilia network runs its own Explorator instance — monitoring local sources, in the local language, for the patterns that only surface at regional level. What they find feeds upward.

Local sources

Each node monitors its own regional and national source tier — RSS feeds, Telegram channels, political accounts in the local language. A narrative seeding in Polish fringe channels is visible to the Polish node before it appears anywhere else.

Upstream intelligence

Every cluster detected by every node flows upstream to Brussels. Cross-border patterns — the same narrative seeding simultaneously in Hungary, Slovakia, and Austria — become visible only when nodes share what they see.

Live Europa Map

The aggregated upstream intelligence from all Explorator instances is what builds the Live Europa Map: a real-time picture of narrative activity across the continent, visible from the centre, steerable from Brussels.
