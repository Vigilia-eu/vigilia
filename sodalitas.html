Sodalitas

Vigilia cannot be built
by two people.
It is designed to be built
by a continent.

The architecture is here. The intelligence layer is running. The editorial infrastructure is operational. What is missing is the network — the journalists, researchers, developers, and institutions that make continental scale real. This is the invitation.

The principle

Infrastructure, not a company.

Vigilia is not being built as a media company with employees, a venture-capital raise, and a product roadmap. That model cannot reach 27 countries in 24 languages at the speed the threat operates. It cannot place a journalist in Warsaw who knows the local political terrain. It cannot run an Explorator instance that reads Hungarian Telegram channels and understands what it is reading. It cannot be in Riga and Lyon and Athens simultaneously.

What can do all of that is a federated network of people and institutions who bring their own expertise, their own geography, and their own languages — and share a common infrastructure, a common intelligence layer, and a common commitment to a functioning European democracy.

You don't join Vigilia as a user. You join as a builder. The network determines how far Vigilia reaches. The collective decides what it becomes. The September 2026 milestone is the first moment where that becomes real — two nodes, one shared intelligence layer, one live demo. The founding participants shape everything that follows.

Who is needed

Six roles. One network.

Vigilia is looking for people and institutions who fit one of six roles. Each brings something the network cannot function without. Each gets something the network is uniquely positioned to provide.

Investigator
Researcher & Analyst
You study disinformation, hybrid threats, political influence operations, or European political dynamics. You produce intelligence that informs counter-strategy — not just academic output.
What you get Explorator intelligence briefings before the story breaks. Cross-border data that no single research organisation has. A direct line from your findings into the publishing infrastructure.
Scriptor
Journalist
You write about European politics, security, or society. You need better intelligence on where stories come from. You want your work to reach beyond one outlet and one language.
What you get Pre-debunk briefings when a coordinated narrative is seeding — before it becomes the story. Machina's distribution infrastructure across 27 countries. The network itself as a source.
Faber
Developer
You build things. Protocol design, node infrastructure, monitoring tooling, data pipelines, front-end. You want to work on something that matters at continental scale with real operational stakes.
What you get A genuine technical challenge — federated architecture at EU scale is not a solved problem. A founding role in the infrastructure layer. Work that is in production, not a side project.
Curator
Node Operator
You are based in a European country and can run the national node — local sources, local language, local publishing. You understand the political terrain in your country and can make editorial decisions in it.
What you get The patria-package: a deployable node instance with Explorator, Machina, and local publishing configured. Full editorial autonomy over your node's output. Direct upstream connection to Brussels.
Consiliarius
Think Tank & Institution
You research European policy, security, or democratic resilience. You have audiences that Vigilia's intelligence reaches. You want operational relevance, not just publication.
What you get Intelligence that connects all four threat layers in a single picture — something no existing institution currently provides. Distribution for your own research through the Vigilia network.
Civis
Local Contributor
You are based somewhere in Europe and you see things. Local political dynamics, regional narratives, patterns in your city or municipality that don't appear in national media yet.
What you get Your local knowledge feeds the continental picture. What you observe in your region becomes upstream intelligence for the Brussels node — the kind of signal that doesn't appear in RSS feeds.
The exchange

What the network requires and returns.

What you bring
  • Geographic presence in a European country
  • Language capability — reading and writing in your national language
  • Domain expertise: journalism, research, technology, policy, or local knowledge
  • Commitment to European democratic resilience as a shared project
  • Willingness to operate within the editorial and ethical framework
  • Time — this is not passive membership
What the network provides
  • Explorator intelligence: early warning before the narrative breaks
  • Machina infrastructure: publishing at scale without building it yourself
  • 27-country distribution through the federated node network
  • Cross-border data: the pattern your country node sees plus what every other node sees
  • A founding role in infrastructure that will operate at continental scale
  • Peers: researchers, journalists, and analysts across Europe working the same problem
Honest state

Where Vigilia is right now.

What is built and running

Explorator (Sentinel) is operational in Phase 1 scope: 877 items monitored, 32 active clusters, real-time alerting, daily digest. Machina pipelines are running with live output. The website exists. The architecture is documented. The intelligence layer is producing.

What is not yet built: the federation at scale. Right now there is one central node. The September 2026 milestone is a two-node live demo — Belgium and the Netherlands, two Explorator instances sharing upstream intelligence, one shared publishing infrastructure. That is the proof of concept that makes the rest of the network credible.
